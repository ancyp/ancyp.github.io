It was preparation as usual, says student

”Past years questions papers helped me a lot in preparing for the examinations, “ says Ancy Philip, a student of CSI Jessie Moses Matriculation and Higher Secondary School, Anna Nagar, and a resident of Villivakkam. She scored 1189 out of 1200 in the State Board Plus Two Board Examinations including centum in Mathematics, Physics and Chemistry, 199 in Computer Science, 192 in English and 198 in French. “I prepared for the exams the usual way. Writing down the formulae and equations was a good experience. I hit the coaching class for Mathematics, Physics and Chemistry,” she said. “My classmates woke up at 4 in the morning to prepare and went to bed at mid-night. I woke up at 6:00 a.m. and went to sleep at 10:00 p.m. For the past one year, I kept away from mobile phone, internet and cable T.V.” “I should thank my School Principal Shyamla Prasad, class and physics teacher Beena Prasad, mother Jancy Lukose, who works as a senior section officer in Integral Coach Factory for their constant support and encouragement,” she said. Her father, Reji Philip, passed away in an accident four years back. “My mother helped me a lot. I was not allowed to visit the nearby shop. She used to drop me at the coaching class. She didn't make me feel the absence of my father. I owe her a lot. I am planning to do Electrical and Electronics Engineering or Electronics and Communication Engineering at Anna University, Chennai. My ambition is to join the Indian Administrative Service and serve the people,” Ancy Philip said. “I wrote all my goals on a sheet of paper and put it in my study room so that I remained focused on achieving them. I was scared and nervous like any XII student. I prayed hard to Lord Jesus. Never lose faith in Lord,” she says. There is no respite from phone calls at home with friends and relatives calling from Chennai, Kerala, London and Dubai to congratulate. I want to thank my classmates R. Harini and R. Priyanka who were very supportive from the start.” “Even if you study for an hour, do it with dedication and commitment. Try not to get distracted while hitting the books. Do your best and leave the rest to the Lord. My teachers used to say that I would make the school proud one-day. To be frank, I never expected this. Another thing I have noticed is most students just blindly mug up without understanding the concept. Students should understand the concept and study,” she says.

Anna University student wins Ford’s Alan Mulally Leadership Scholarship

Ford Fund manages the programme as part of its global Ford Blue Oval Scholars initiative Ancy Philip, an engineering student from the department of Computer Science of Chennai’s Anna University has become the first Indian recipient of Alan Mulally Leadership in Engineering Scholarship in India. The $10,000 scholarship is part of Ford Motor Company’s global initiative to honor the outstanding service of Alan Mulally, the company’s former CEO. Starting from this year onwards the Alan Mulally Leadership in Engineering Scholarship programme will award 10 one-time $10,000 scholarships each year to students at partner universities around the world. Ford Fund will manage the programme as part of its global Ford Blue Oval Scholars initiative. Scholarship winners will be connected with the company’s corporate science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) strategic initiative that aims to build a new generation of leaders in these disciplines.

Ancy's Vroom Factor

For someone who frankly admits that she doesn’t have a lot of “fun” in the traditional sense, Ancy Philip, winner of a $10,000 Alan Mulally Scholarship from automobile giant Ford, comes across as quite a lively and interesting person. Ancy, surprisingly, is not studying anything directly related to automobiles. She is a student of Computer Science Engineering at Anna University, Chennai. The win is not something that happened overnight, but is rather the result of Ancy’s long-held interest in incorporating Computer Science technology into cars. The guidelines for the scholarship required three qualities — leadership, educational achieveme nts and extra-curricular activities which show a passion for automobiles. “Over the past month, I’ve been working on a project dealing with smartcars, specifically on the V to V communication between cars. A competition was held at IIT Madras, where 20 projects were shortlisted from 120 students for the next round. This was an idea I had a long time ago, which I had wanted to execute,” said Ancy. Ford has announced that scholarship winners will be connected with a strategic initiative by the car maker, aimed at building a new generation of leaders in the key disciplines of science, technology, engineering and math (STEM). When Ancy talks about this aspect of the scholarship, one can hear the excitement in her voice. Knowing Ford, they’re likely to hold up their end of the bargain — especially given the amount of time, energy and money they pour into their Research and Development. Incidentally, Ancy had worked on another innovative project which involved developing a program for predicting prices of vegetables in India by utilizing prices over the last five years, and a whole lot of other data. With such passion for her field, it is no surprise that Ancy plans to keep studying after she graduates. அண்ணா பல்கலைக்கழகத்தில் கம்ப்யூட்டர் சயின்ஸ் நான்காம் ஆண்டு படித்து வரும் மாணவி அன்சி பிலிப்பிற்கு ஃபோர்டு பவுண்டேஷன் ஆலன் முலா லீடர்ஷிப் ஸ்காலர்ஷிப் கிடைத்துள்ளது. இந்த உதவித் தொகை பெறும் முதல் இந்திய மாணவி இவர். ஒவ்வொரு ஆண்டும் ஃபோர்டு பவுண்டேஷன் உலக அளவில் இன்ஜினீயரிங் துறையில் சிறப்பாக செயல்பட்டு வரும் மாணவர்களை தேர்ந்தெடுத்து அவர்களுக்கு உதவித்தொகை வழங்கி வருகிறது. இந்த ஆண்டு ஃபோர்டு பவுண்டேஷன் ஆலன் முலா லீடர்ஷிப் ஸ்காலர்ஷிப் (Alan Mulally Leadership in Engineering Scholarship) பெற அண்ணா பல்கலைக் கழகத்தில் கம்ப்யூட்டர் சயின்ஸ் நான்காம் ஆண்டு மாணவி அன்சி பிலிப் தேர்வு செய்யப்பட்டுள்ளார். அவருக்கு உதவித் தொகையாக 10 ஆயிரம் டாலர் கிடைக்கும். இந்த உதவித் தொகை பெறும் முதல் இந்திய மாணவி இவர். அண்ணா பல்கலைக்கழகத் துணைவேந்தர் எம். ராஜாராம் முன்னிலையில் நடைபெற்ற நிகழ்ச்சியில் போர்டு இந்தியா தலைவர் மற்றும் நிர்வாக இயக்குநர் நிஜில் ஹாரீஸ், போர்டு மோட்டார் நிறுவனத்தின் நிர்வாக இயக்குநர் டேவிட் துபன்ஸ்கி, ஆகியோர் அன்சிக்கு இந்த உதவித் தொகை வழங்குவதற்கான சான்றிதழை அளித்தனர். எப்படி கிடைத்தது பொறியியல் துறையின் தலைமைப்பண்புக்கான உதவித்தொகை என்ற கேள்வியோடு அன்சி பிலிப்பை சந்தித்தோம்.. source: http://puthiyathalaimurai.com/component/content/article/10-pt-last-week/2026-2015-10-19-09-21-45
